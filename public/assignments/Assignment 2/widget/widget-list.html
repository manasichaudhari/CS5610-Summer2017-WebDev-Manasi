These Ancient Texts Might Help Us Predict the Next Massive Solar Storm

That’s what a team of Japanese scientists are hoping. They are browsing through ancient documents to see what kinds of solar storms folks living back then saw and recorded. They think that some of these documented auroral events could be from “great magnetic storms” coming from strong solar events. If so, these texts could help us understand just how frequently these storms occur, seeing as they’d be far more harmful today given our reliance on electricity.

Beautiful phenomenon of Northern lights

To fully appreciate the glory and grandeur of this celestial display, which is also known as the aurora borealis, you have to settle beneath the ever-changing lights and watch them curve and curl, slither and flicker.  "I was camping, just lying out in a field in a sleeping bag on a late September night and looking up at the stars," said Terry Onsager, a physicist with the National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration's Space Weather Prediction Center in Boulder, Colorado.
